1) Introduction to the scene
The novel opens with a tense prologue where a man is desperately fleeing his pursuers and leaps into the harbor in Djibouti, hinting at a dangerous past. The story then shifts to the present day in Pittsburgh, introducing Greg Abimbola, a quiet and unassuming language teacher at the prestigious Calderhill Academy. His seemingly peaceful life is disrupted when a wealthy and influential parent confronts him for giving her daughter a 'B' in Russian. This initial confrontation sets the stage, highlighting the class tensions and high-stakes environment of the elite school, which soon becomes the backdrop for a much darker event: a murder.

2) Themes Highlighted
"A Quiet Teacher" explores several complex themes:

Past vs. Present: The central theme is the inescapable nature of one's past. Greg has tried to build a new, quiet life, but his former life in international espionage inevitably catches up with him.

Identity and Belonging: Greg's identity is multi-layered and often conflicting. He is a Black, British man of Nigerian descent living in America, a former spy turned teacher, and a deeply religious man struggling with his sexuality. The novel examines his struggle to reconcile these different facets of himself.

Class and Privilege: The setting of Calderhill Academy serves as a microcosm of societal inequality. The novel critiques the entitlement and ambition of the wealthy elite, who believe their money and influence can bend any rule.

Secrets and Deception: Nearly every character in the book is hiding something. The narrative is built on layers of secrets, both personal and professional, driving the plot forward as Greg works to uncover the truth.

3) Character Analysis
Greg Abimbola is a compelling and unconventional protagonist.

A Man of Contradictions: On the surface, he is a dedicated, mild-mannered teacher. However, beneath this facade lies a highly skilled and lethal former intelligence operative. This duality makes him an unpredictable and fascinating character.

Internal Conflict: Greg is deeply religious and constantly grapples with guilt over his past actions and his sexuality. This internal struggle adds significant psychological depth to his character, making his motivations complex and relatable.

Reluctant Hero: He is not seeking to return to his old life. Instead, he is forced to use his dormant skills to protect himself and his students when danger arrives at the school. He is a man who wants peace but is exceptionally capable of violence when necessary.

4) Literary Devices and Styles
Adam Oyebanji employs a unique blend of genres and styles:

Genre Blending: The novel is a hybrid of a cozy murder mystery and a high-stakes spy thriller. It combines the "whodunit" element of a classic mystery set in a closed community (the school) with the action, suspense, and global intrigue of a spy story.

First-Person Narration: The story is told from Greg's perspective, which allows the reader direct access to his internal thoughts, fears, and conflicts. This narrative choice effectively builds suspense as the reader only knows what Greg knows, and it deepens the character study.

Pacing: The pacing is deliberately measured at the start, establishing the characters and the academic setting, much like a traditional mystery. However, it accelerates significantly as elements from Greg's past intrude, injecting the fast-paced action of a thriller into the narrative.
